{Leisl's Professional Resumé}

Leisl Schrader, pronounced /ˈliːsl/ /ˈʃreɪdər/ using the International Phonetic Alphabet,* is a geeky, misfit, wildly imaginative, malcontent creative multitasker, with 15+ years experience in design, technology**, art, media, marketing, and advertising; a specialist in branding and logo design; and an enthusiast of Agile Design, Design Thinking, and the creative process.

She is also a maker/crafter/artisan, entrepreneur, public speaker, streamer, podcaster, interior designer, photographer, writer/blogger, alt-fashionista, cosplayer, introvert, neurodivergent, and female, among other things. Her favorite topics of discussion are graphic design, branding, art, creativity, entrepreneurship, and mental health.

* Audio guide here.

In layman's terms, it is pronounced like “LEESS” as in the “lease” on your apartment (with an “s” sound, not a “z” sound); “ul” as in “full”; “SHRAY” that rhymes with “tray”; and “dur” as in “dirt.”
Say it with me: “LEASE-full TRAY-dirt.” Remove the “f” and the last “t”, then replace the first “t” with an “sh,” and voila — you can now correctly pronounce “Leisl Schrader.”
Incorrect pronunciations of “Leisl” include, but are not limited to: “Leez-ul,” “Lace-ul,” “Lice-ul,” “Lies-ul,” “Lies-elle,””Lice-elle,””Lace-elle,“ “Lease-elle,” “Leez-elle,” “Lisa,” “Leslie,” “Lacey,” “Lucy,” and “Lucille.”

** Work history notwithstanding, Leisl is not currently a Web developer, software engineer, tech wizard, code ninja, programming guru, nor an Internet rockstar; more of an artist, really.
